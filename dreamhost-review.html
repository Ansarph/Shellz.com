Who DreamHost is best for

DreamHost can be a good choice when its plan structure matches your website type. Before choosing, think about whether you are building a simple portfolio, a small business website, a WordPress blog, a coupon site, a WooCommerce store, or a high-traffic publication.

Best for

  • WordPress users who want a long-established hosting company
  • Beginners who want free SSL and domain options on eligible terms
  • Bloggers and small business sites
  • Users comparing longer money-back and promo terms
  • People who do not require traditional cPanel

Not best for

  • Users who specifically want cPanel
  • People who need the cheapest possible short-term monthly price
  • Projects that require data center location choice outside DreamHost’s setup
  • Advanced users who want unmanaged root server control on shared hosting
  • Beginners who want every support channel available 24/7 by phone

What I like about DreamHost

DreamHost has clear strengths, but it is not the perfect host for every user. The right way to judge it is to compare features, support, renewal cost, and upgrade path against your actual website plan.

Strengths

  • Established hosting provider
  • WordPress-friendly plan options

Watch-outs
