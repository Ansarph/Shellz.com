Buying guide

How to choose web hosting

1. Match hosting to your site

Shared hosting is usually enough for small blogs and simple business sites. WordPress hosting helps if your site runs WordPress. VPS or cloud hosting fits larger, faster-growing projects.

2. Check real long-term cost

Introductory prices can look cheap. Compare renewal prices, backup costs, security add-ons, email costs, domain renewal, and migration fees.

3. Look for performance basics

Prioritize modern storage, caching, CDN options, uptime history, and the ability to upgrade resources without moving your whole site.

4. Review support and security

For beginners, support quality can matter more than raw specifications. Look for SSL, backups, malware tools, and clear help channels.
