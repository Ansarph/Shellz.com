Company background and trust signals

COIN.HOST says it has operated since 2011 and is provided by Solar Communications GmbH, a privately owned Swiss company and RIPE NCC member. The company publishes a physical Zurich address, a data-center address, policy documents, an SLA, a security statement, payment information, an affiliate program, a knowledge base, and customer reviews.

The provider also claims more than 25,000 customers and over 100 hosting solutions. These are provider-reported figures, not independently audited figures verified by Shellz.

Dedicated servers

Dedicated servers are the core COIN.HOST product. The provider advertises more than 60 bare-metal configurations, from lower-cost machines to high-performance clusters and GPU-equipped systems. Dedicated hosting gives the customer exclusive access to the server hardware rather than sharing CPU and memory with unrelated tenants.

Crypto-friendly billing

Customers can pay with Bitcoin and many other digital currencies, alongside cards, PayPal, and bank wire.

Swiss connectivity

The provider highlights a multihomed network and direct connection to SwissIX for routing efficiency and resilience.

DDoS protection

COIN.HOST advertises always-on DDoS protection with unmetered mitigation for dedicated servers.

Remote management

Some customer reviews and product descriptions mention KVM/IPMI-style remote administration, useful for bare-metal recovery and control.

Hardware replacement SLA

The published SLA says covered hardware should be replaced within one hour after the problem is identified, excluding RAID rebuild time.

Technical responsibility

A dedicated server offers control, but the customer must secure, update, monitor, and back up the operating system and applications.

Confidential computing servers

In 2026, COIN.HOST added confidential-computing servers using AMD Secure Encrypted Virtualization. The provider describes hardware-enforced encryption for virtual-machine memory, combined with encrypted storage and user-controlled keys.

This product is aimed at workloads where normal disk encryption is not enough. Examples include sensitive databases, fintech services, healthcare workloads, intellectual property, SaaS applications, and blockchain infrastructure. The key idea is that data remains protected while being processed in memory—not only while stored on disk.

Shellz has not independently validated the exact AMD SEV implementation, key-management workflow, performance overhead, or compliance suitability. Organizations with regulated data should conduct their own technical and legal assessment rather than relying on a marketing page alone.

Swiss data center, privacy, and compliance claims

COIN.HOST says its infrastructure is located in an Interxion data center near Zurich. Published features include redundant power, multihomed connectivity, physical security controls, renewable energy, DDoS protection, and direct SwissIX connectivity.

The provider says the facility operates according to ITILv3, ISO 27001 and ISO 22301, and that FINMA Circular 07/8 compliance is externally audited. These statements relate to the data-center and operational environment. Customers requiring formal certification evidence should request current certificates and determine whether they cover the exact service being purchased.

Swiss hosting can provide meaningful jurisdictional and data-residency benefits, but it does not create absolute anonymity. COIN.HOST's terms allow identity, residency, contact, or intended-use verification for security, fraud prevention, sanctions, or compliance reasons.

Security features

The official security statement describes firewalls, DDoS protection, SSL/TLS, intrusion detection, authenticator-app 2FA, anti-malware software, internal VPN use, security audits, and automated backups for eligible plans.

These controls are positive trust signals, but a dedicated or confidential server still requires customer-side security. You should plan for operating-system patching, SSH hardening, firewall rules, application security, monitoring, off-site backups, secret management, and incident response.

Payment methods and crypto billing

COIN.HOST accepts a broad range of cryptocurrencies and digital assets, including BTC, BCH, XRP, ETH, Dash, and LTC, together with PayPal, major cards, and bank wire. This flexibility is one of the provider's main differentiators.

Crypto payments can be convenient for international customers, but buyers should consider blockchain fees, price volatility, exchange-rate conversion, transaction-finality delays, accounting records, and the provider's non-refund policy after a billing period starts.

Terms, billing, and cancellation risks

COIN.HOST's current terms state that contracts generally have a minimum one-month term and renew automatically unless terminated before the current term expires. Services are normally billed monthly unless another arrangement applies.

The terms also state that payments are non-refundable once the billing period has begun unless otherwise agreed. Prices are usually stated in Swiss francs, and customers are responsible for taxes, conversion charges, transaction fees, and payment-provider delays.

Overdue services can be suspended quickly. For instant servers, the terms describe immediate suspension when overdue and deletion shortly afterward. This makes timely payment and independent backups especially important.

Backups: the customer remains responsible

Even where a plan includes or offers backups, the terms say the customer is solely responsible for maintaining backups and that provider backups are supplied on a best-effort basis without warranty. Important data should therefore be copied to an independent location and restoration should be tested.

SLA and uptime

The published SLA guarantees 99.9% monthly server availability, excluding scheduled maintenance. It also describes 100% electricity and HVAC availability, internal-network expectations, hardware replacement within one hour after identification, and a paid Gold SLA with a 30-minute support-response target.

Published credits generally equal 5% of the monthly fee for each qualifying hour, subject to category and monthly limits. A support ticket must be opened, and claims must be filed within the stated period. The SLA excludes customer-caused problems, software failures, server overload, DDoS attacks, hacking, force majeure, overdue accounts, and policy violations.

Acceptable-use restrictions

Privacy-focused hosting is not unrestricted hosting. COIN.HOST prohibits spam, malware, phishing, unauthorized access, copyright infringement, harmful content, network abuse, proxy/VPN services unless permitted, HYIP programs, and other unlawful or high-risk uses.

Cryptocurrency mining is explicitly prohibited on both virtual and dedicated servers. This is a crucial detail because the brand's crypto focus could otherwise mislead some customers into assuming that mining is allowed.

Support and customer reviews

The company advertises 24/7 multilingual support. Its public review page contains customer comments about dedicated-server stability, setup speed, pricing, and crypto payment. Because those reviews are hosted by the provider, they should be considered a useful signal—but not a substitute for independent review platforms or hands-on testing.

A future Shellz test should measure ticket response time, provisioning time, network latency, DDoS response, hardware-replacement handling, cancellation, backup recovery, and crypto-payment processing.

Who should choose COIN.HOST?

COIN.HOST is a strong shortlist candidate when you need Swiss dedicated infrastructure, crypto payments, privacy-conscious operations, DDoS protection, or confidential computing. It is particularly relevant to technically capable customers who understand server management and want jurisdictional or payment flexibility.

It is probably unnecessary for a small WordPress blog, portfolio, or local business site. Those projects will usually be easier and cheaper on standard shared or managed WordPress hosting.

Final verdict

COIN.HOST is a credible specialist provider with a clear niche: Swiss, privacy-focused, crypto-friendly infrastructure. It publishes more operational and legal detail than many small hosting brands, including company identity, data-center information, security claims, an SLA, terms, acceptable-use rules, and payment options.

The provider is not anonymous, risk-free, or unrestricted. Read the non-refund terms, backup responsibility, sanctions and verification clauses, acceptable-use restrictions, and service-deletion timelines carefully.
